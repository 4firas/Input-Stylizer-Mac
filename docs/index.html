what_does_it_do.txt

how it works

you type something. you hit Return. instead of sending immediately, your text goes to an AI model, gets rewritten in the style you picked, and then that's what gets sent.

you don't press anything extra. you don't paste anything. you don't open a separate app. the app lives in your menu bar, takes 0% of your attention, and turns every message into a slightly better version of itself.

(so: it's not a keylogger. it just does keylogger things, but with taste.)

install_instructions.sh

install in 90 seconds

  1. go to the releases page and download SystemWideStylizer.app.zip
  2. unzip it and move the .app to your Applications folder
  3. open it. macOS will warn you it's from an unidentified developer. click Open Anyway in System Settings → Privacy & Security
  4. go to System Settings → Privacy & Security → Accessibility and toggle on SystemWideStylizer
  5. click the menu bar icon (the dotted-underline "abc" thing), pick a style and a provider
  6. type anywhere. press Return. witness transformation.

tip: if the menu bar icon shows up grayed out, accessibility isn't on yet. toggle the app off and back on once — that fixes it permanently.

FAQ

does it send my text somewhere?

only to the AI provider you picked in settings. nothing leaves your machine otherwise. no telemetry, no analytics, no "phone home" stuff. the entire app is ~1.1mb and you can read every line of it.

does it work in [insert app here]?

if [insert app here] has a text field that accepts regular keyboard input, yes. works in Discord, Slack, Messages, Notes, browsers, terminal — basically everywhere except password fields (which it should refuse to touch, and does).

why does the menu bar icon look sad?

you don't have Accessibility permission yet. grant it in System Settings → Privacy & Security → Accessibility, then toggle the app on. the icon will light up.

can i add my own style?

yes — pick Custom Prompt from the style menu, then write whatever you want. want your messages to read like a wikipedia article? an instagram caption? a guy named jeff? go for it.

will apple kill this in a future macOS update?

maybe. accessibility APIs are private-feeling but the ones we use have been stable since 10.10. if apple ever breaks them, we'll find another way. probably.

why does the icon have a dotted underline?

it's an SF Symbol called textformat.abc.dottedunderline and it's the perfect icon for this. fight us.
